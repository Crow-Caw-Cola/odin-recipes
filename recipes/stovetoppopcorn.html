Stovetop Popcorn

Description

Popcorn is a great snack because, as long as it's not covered in stuff that's bad for you, it makes a much healthier alternative to potato chips and the like. And if you make it yourself, it's much cheaper than store-bought snacks as well!

This is a simple yet tasty recipe for making popcorn on the stove. In just a few minutes, you can have a hot, tasty snack that's also rich in fiber!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup popcorn kernels
  • 2 Tbsp oil

    • I recommend olive oil for extra flavor, but standard canola or vegetable oil works just fine.

  • Salt (to taste)
  • Ajinomoto/MSG (optional; to taste)

Steps

  1. Add the oil and two (yes, just two) popcorn kernels to the frying pan and heat over a medium heat. I also like to add a bit of salt to the oil as well as a tiny dash of Ajinomoto/MSG. Cover the frying pan with a lid.
  2. As soon as both of the kernels have popped, turn off the heat, add the rest of the kernels to the pan, and cover. Let sit for one minute.
  3. Heat the pan again over a medium high heat. Keep the pan covered with the lid, but leave a very tiny gap so excess steam can escape.
  4. Once the popping of the kernels dies down to around one pop every second or so, remove the pan from the heat. If you've ever made microwave popcorn, etc., it's the same idea.
  5. Add the popcorn to a bowl and salt to taste. I personally like to add a layer of popcorn, lightly salt that layer, than add the next.
